Temperature dependence of Eg

  • Electron-phonon coupling - silicon
  • Thermal dilatation - perovskite

J. Phys. Chem. Lett. 10 1368–1373 (2019)

For most semiconductors the thermal dilatation is negligible and the bandgap decreases with temperature, but for perovskite the thermal dilatation of the lattice is extraordinary high, and overrules the electron-phonon coupling effect.

In order to model temperature dependence of PCE, exponential Urbach band tailing was taken into account.
Perovskite Urbach energy can be written as a sum of two components - static component (intrinsic disorder of the material), and phonon contribution (how vibrations influence the electrical properties). $\theta_E$ - Einstein phonon temperature.
